The Day-1 Program
Debate: Artificial Inelegance vs. The Future of Human Resources

Shaping the Future of Human Resources in Government and Business

Moving forward with our commitment to support the advancement of the human resource management in line with global standards, Datamatix presents the summit, a dynamic platform for exploring the future of human capital amid rapid AI advancements. The summit aims to empower organizations to adopt innovative HR strategies, enhancing human capital competitiveness in AI advancements and digital era.

The debate, “Artificial Intelligence vs. The Future of Human Resources,” will address opportunities, challenges, and potential conflicts. It will explore whether AI will replace HR functions, how AI is reshaping corporate HR strategies, and how organizations can balance AI-driven automation with human-centric workforce management. Additionally, the discussion will highlight emerging trends, ethical considerations, and the evolving role of HR professionals in an AI-driven workplace.

The technological and managerial debate at the summit will serve as a key forum for examining the intersection of AI and HR. Discussions will cover digital transformation strategies in human capital management from both scientific and technological perspectives, addressing challenges posed by emerging technologies particularly AI-driven automation and the evolving job landscape. Additionally, the sessions will explore best practices in talent acquisition and workforce development in the era of “Artificial Intelligence”

As AI reshapes the workforce, recruitment strategies must evolve to meet new demands. Automation is reducing the need for traditional roles, pushing organizations to restructure HR strategies and prioritize expertise in strategic analysis, creativity, and decision-making.

Smart recruitment tools are reducing reliance on large recruitment teams. The rise of remote work and the gig economy further necessitates adaptive HR frameworks, as companies increasingly depend on freelancers and contractors, leveraging AI to manage decentralized teams effectively. In this context, the summit will provide a comprehensive vision for organizations to enhance adaptability, develop advanced organizational infrastructures, and implement effective strategies to sustain human capital.

As a global platform, the Datamatix 31st Human Resources Summit, will bring together decision-makers and industry experts to exchange insights on best practices in human capital management, digital governance, and smart recruitment. The overall impact of AI-driven transformations depends on how effectively organizations balance technology adoption with sustainable HR policies. This necessitates a strategic shift toward investing in human capital development, ensuring workforce adaptability, and preparing for future challenges.

Vision and Objectives

The debate aims to explore how AI technologies are reshaping human resources management and developing sustainable, forward-thinking strategies. Key objectives include:

  • Building an AI roadmap for sustainable HR management.
  • Assessing AI’s impact on recruitment, talent acquisition, and human capital.
  • Leveraging big data, analytics, and HR Tech for better decision-making.
  • Developing leadership skills for digital workforce transformation.
  • Overcoming digital transformation challenges with strategic solutions.
  • Redefining wages, incentives, and performance evaluation in an AI-driven era.
  • Exploring automation, robotics, and machine learning in HR functions.
  • Enhancing governance, transparency, and ethics in AI-driven HR.
  • Integrating AI into workforce diversity, remote work, and inclusivity.
  • Understanding AI’s impact on future job roles, labor laws, and skill gaps.
  • Fostering HR innovation, leadership, and tech integration.
  • Showcasing global best practices in HR, development, and employee experience.
  • Bridging public-private sector dialogues on workforce and economic shifts.
  • Emphasizing emotional intelligence in future team management.
  • Promoting flexible work models for productivity and innovation.
  • Creating training programs for future-ready skills.
  • Strengthening digital leadership for institutional excellence.
  • Cultivating a corporate culture of creativity and innovation.
  • Enhancing employee experience through AI-driven strategies.
  • Shaping future talent management and addressing tech skill shortages.
  • Prioritizing employee mental health and well-being strategies.
  • Defining the “digital employee” and its role in sustainability.
  • Aligning HR policies with sustainability and corporate responsibility.
  • Measuring ROI in HR programs and training initiatives.
  • Strengthening academia-industry partnerships for future talent.
